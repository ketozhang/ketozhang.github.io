The coriolis force is defined as,

\[ 2m\dot {\mathbf r} \times \boldsymbol \Omega\]

  • \(\dot{\mathbf r}\) : velocity vector of the object relative to the rotating frame

Note that this is very similar to the force induced by a magnetic field ($F_B = q $) that causes charged particles to spiral around a direction of the \(B\)-field. The exact same spiral occurs with the coriolis force but at a much weaker extent.
